Periodic Steady-State Control of a Handkerchief-Spinning Task
Using a Parallel Anti-Parallelogram Tendon-driven Wrist

Abstract

Spinning flexible objects, exemplified by traditional Chinese handkerchief performances, demands periodic steady-state motions under nonlinear dynamics with frictional contacts and boundary constraints. Humans learn this skill almost intuitively after limited training, whereas humanoid robots lack both suitable wrist dexterity and effective strategies for initial and stable control. To address this gap, we develop a parallel anti-parallelogram tendon-driven wrist with rapid dynamic response and propose a simplified control-oriented model to capture the initiation dynamics. Using this model, we optimize initiation strategies, enabling robust transitions from rest to stable periodic spinning. Our results demonstrate that combining wrist dexterity with model-based control provides humanoid robots with new capabilities for manipulating flexible objects in dynamic tasks.
